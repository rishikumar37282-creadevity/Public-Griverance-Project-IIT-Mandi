⚙️ How your complaint is processed

A transparent look at the AI pipeline behind this portal.

STEP 1

Text → tokens

Your complaint is lower-cased and split into words; each word becomes a number the model knows (vocabulary of ~1,000+ terms learned from real grievances).

STEP 2

Bidirectional LSTM

A recurrent neural network reads your complaint forwards and backwards, building context-aware features for every word.

STEP 3

Attention

The model computes which words matter most (“stopped”, “no response”, “elderly”) and focuses on them — you see this as highlights in your result.

STEP 4

Two predictions

Parallel classifier heads predict the department (15 classes) and the urgency (Low / Medium / High / Critical) simultaneously.

STEP 5

Urgency score 0–100

A rule-based oracle adds an explainable score from severity keywords, durations, vulnerable groups, and prior escalations.

STEP 6

Action plan

Your urgency level maps to routing, response deadlines, and an escalation path — plus concrete next steps for you.

🧪 Limitations: trained on ~4,800 English complaints across 15 departments. Very short texts, other languages, or unseen topics reduce accuracy. The urgency score is advisory — officers make the final call. For life-threatening emergencies always call 112 directly.
